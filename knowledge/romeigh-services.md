## DENR Regulatory Framework

### Hazardous Waste Regulations
Under DAO 2013-22 (Hazardous Waste Management), Philippine industries must:
- Register as hazardous waste generators with DENR-EMB
- Obtain a Hazardous Waste Generator ID
- Maintain a hazardous waste storage area (maximum 90-day on-site storage)
- Use only DENR-accredited transporters and treatment facilities
- Submit Hazardous Waste Manifests for every shipment
- Report quarterly using the prescribed format

Common hazardous wastes in Philippine industry:
- Used lubricating oils (M503)
- Spent solvents (M001-M007)
- Electroplating wastes (M201-M204)
- Hospital/pharmaceutical wastes (H-coded)
- Heavy metal-containing wastes

### Wastewater Discharge Standards
Under DAO 2021-08 and RA 9275 (Clean Water Act):
- Effluent must meet Class C water quality standards before discharge
- BOD limit: 50 mg/L for most industries
- TSS limit: 70 mg/L
- pH range: 6.0-9.0
- Heavy metals must meet specific limits
- Dischargers must obtain a Discharge Permit from DENR-EMB

### Air Emissions
Under RA 8749 (Clean Air Act) and DAO 2000-81:
- Boiler and combustion facilities need a Permit to Operate
- Emission testing required every two years
- Pollution Control Officers (PCOs) must be registered with DENR-EMB

## Common Compliance Questions

**Q: Do I need a Pollution Control Officer (PCO)?**
A: Yes, if your facility has a boiler, incinerator, wastewater treatment plant, or generates significant pollution, Philippine regulations require a registered PCO. Contact ROMEIGH to discuss your compliance needs.

**Q: How do I get a Hazardous Waste Generator ID?**
A: Submit an application to your DENR-EMB regional office with a list of wastes generated, facility layout, and waste storage plan. ROMEIGH can guide you through this process.

**Q: What happens if I dispose of hazardous waste improperly?**
A: Violations under RA 6969 (Toxic Substances and Hazardous Waste Act) carry penalties of PHP 10,000 to PHP 50,000 per day of violation, plus possible criminal liability for company officers.

**Q: How often do I need to submit compliance reports?**
A: Hazardous waste manifests are required per shipment. Self-Monitoring Reports (SMR) are typically quarterly. Annual Compliance Reports are submitted to DENR-EMB by March 31 each year.
